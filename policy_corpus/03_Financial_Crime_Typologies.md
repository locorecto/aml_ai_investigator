# Common Financial Crime Typologies

## Structuring (Smurfing)
**Description**: Repeated transactions just below reporting thresholds.
**Indicators**:
- Multiple cash deposits under thresholds
- Activity spread across branches or days
**Risk Considerations**:
- Intent to evade reporting requirements

## Rapid Movement of Funds
**Description**: Funds quickly moved in and out of accounts.
**Indicators**:
- Short holding periods
- High velocity wires or transfers
**Risk Considerations**:
- Potential layering or laundering

## Use of High-Risk Jurisdictions
**Description**: Transactions involving jurisdictions with elevated AML risk.
**Indicators**:
- Wires to/from sanctioned or high-risk countries
- No business justification
**Risk Considerations**:
- Sanctions evasion or illicit finance

## Shell or Front Companies
**Description**: Entities with minimal legitimate operations.
**Indicators**:
- High transaction volume with little operational footprint
- Shared addresses or officers
**Risk Considerations**:
- Concealment of beneficial ownership

## Unusual Loan Activity
**Description**: Loans paid down rapidly or inconsistently.
**Indicators**:
- Early repayments without clear source of funds
**Risk Considerations**:
- Integration of illicit proceeds

## Credit Card Misuse
**Description**: Abnormal card usage patterns.
**Indicators**:
- Excessive velocity or cross-border usage
**Risk Considerations**:
- Fraud or laundering via card networks
